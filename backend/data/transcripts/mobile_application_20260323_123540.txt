Q1: Can you describe the initial inspiration or problem that led you to develop this mobile application?

A1: I built it after watching people bounce between calendars, notes, and task apps just to manage a simple day, and it was clear the tools were creating as much friction as they solved. I wanted to design something that quietly unified all of that so staying organized felt natural instead of like extra work.

Q2: How did you go about identifying and integrating the key features that would address the friction points you observed among existing tools?

A2: I interviewed users to pinpoint the exact moments where their workflow broke down, then built only the features that directly removed those pain points. From there, I integrated them through a single, context‑aware timeline so everything felt cohesive instead of like a pile of add‑ons.

Q3: Can you share an example of a specific feature that emerged from your user interviews and how it transformed the user experience?

A3: One feature that came directly from interviews was a “context window” that automatically grouped tasks based on where the user was and what they were doing, instead of forcing them to sort things manually. Once we added it, users said the app finally felt like it was working *with* them, not demanding extra effort just to stay organized.
